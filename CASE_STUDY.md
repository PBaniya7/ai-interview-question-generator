# InterviewAce AI - Product Case Study

## Overview

InterviewAce AI is an MVP designed to help job seekers prepare for interviews more efficiently by generating personalized interview questions based on role, company, and experience level.

---

## Problem Statement

Many candidates spend hours researching interview questions across websites, forums, and videos.

The process is fragmented and time-consuming.

Candidates often do not know:

- Which questions are most relevant
- What topics to prioritize
- How to structure preparation

As a result, preparation is inefficient and confidence is lower during interviews.

---

## Target User

### Primary User

Mid-career professionals preparing for interviews.

Examples:

- Product Managers
- Business Analysts
- Software Engineers
- Data Analysts

### User Goals

- Prepare faster
- Focus on relevant questions
- Increase interview confidence

---

## User Journey

### Before

Candidate searches:

- Google
- LinkedIn
- YouTube
- Blogs

Collects information manually.

Preparation takes several hours.

### After

Candidate enters:

- Role
- Experience
- Company

System instantly generates:

- Relevant interview questions
- Preparation guidance
- Suggested focus areas

Preparation becomes structured and faster.

---

## MVP Features

### Included

✓ Role Input

✓ Experience Input

✓ Company Input

✓ Interview Question Generation

✓ Preparation Recommendations

---

## Features Deliberately Excluded

To keep the MVP focused, the following were excluded:

- Resume Upload
- Mock Interviews
- Voice Interaction
- Performance Scoring
- Learning Dashboard

These features would be evaluated in future releases.

---

## Success Metrics

### User Metrics

- Number of sessions generated
- Repeat usage rate
- User satisfaction score

### Business Metrics

- Time saved per candidate
- Candidate engagement

---

## Risks

### Risk 1

Generated questions may be too generic.

Mitigation:

Improve personalization logic.

### Risk 2

Users may expect company-specific insights.

Mitigation:

Future integration with AI models.

## Key Learnings

Building a focused MVP is more effective than attempting to solve every user problem at once.

Starting with a narrow use case enables faster validation and iteration.
